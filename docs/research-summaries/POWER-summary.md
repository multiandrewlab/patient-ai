# Summary: The Energy Nexus of Artificial Intelligence: Power Constraints, Scaling Timelines, and the Thermodynamics of Labor Replacement

## Source
- File: `docs/constraints/POWER.md`
- Word count: approximately 5,800
- Primary topic: The physical infrastructure bottlenecks — primarily electrical power and water — that constrain the pace at which AI can scale toward economic labor replacement, and the timelines and technologies needed to lift those constraints.

---

## Core Arguments

- **The primary constraint on AI rollout is thermodynamic, not computational.** As AI transitions to general-purpose cognitive engines, the abstraction of software from physical resources has "violently collapsed." Power grids and water supplies are now the binding chokepoints — not algorithms, chips, or talent.

- **Power demand has structurally broken from its historical baseline.** US five-year peak demand forecasts surged six-fold between 2022 and 2025 (from 24 GW to 166 GW), driven overwhelmingly by AI data centres. The era of flat power demand is definitively over, requiring infrastructure to be built at more than six times recent historical rates.

- **AI data centres impose a novel, unyielding load profile that existing grids were never designed for.** Unlike human-patterned industrial demand, AI inference clusters run at 80–100% load factors continuously — equivalent to one or more mid-sized cities per campus, around the clock. Intermittent renewables are fundamentally mismatched to this demand without prohibitively expensive storage.

- **Water is an equally binding but under-discussed constraint.** The energy-efficiency optimisation of evaporative cooling directly worsens water consumption. Hyperscale facilities can use up to 5 million gallons per day, and the 2024 US Data Center Energy Usage Report projects on-site water use will grow 2–4x between 2023 and 2028.

- **Regulatory and interconnection systems are paralysed by the speed mismatch.** Data centres can be financed and built in months; transmission upgrades take years. The result is a congested interconnection queue and a landmark regulatory conflict (the Talen-Amazon FERC decision) that closed off the "behind-the-meter" strategy for co-locating data centres with existing power plants.

- **Federal intervention is now treating AI power as a national security imperative.** The Trump administration's 2025 "AI Action Plan" and companion executive orders explicitly bypass National Environmental Policy Act reviews, Clean Water Act permitting, and local cost-sharing disputes to force rapid infrastructure permitting.

- **Small Modular Reactors are the intended mid-term solution but carry serious execution risk.** Target: commercial deployment by 2030. Reality: First-of-a-Kind cost premiums, many partnerships lack binding power purchase agreements, and delays could push meaningful nuclear contributions to 2033–2035.

- **Replacing human cognitive labour at scale hits a thermodynamic wall that cannot be solved from the supply side alone.** The brain runs on 20 watts; the most powerful supercomputer needs 21 megawatts to match it. The "Erasi Equation" shows replacing all human intelligence with current silicon would require power levels approximately 1,000 times total US generating capacity. The only viable path is radical "Intelligence per Watt" improvement through algorithmic efficiency and neuromorphic hardware.

- **The Jevons Paradox (rebound effect) means efficiency gains will not reduce total energy demand.** Historical precedent: a thousandfold gain in hardware efficiency over the past decade was matched by a thousandfold increase in total training energy due to induced demand. Economic growth catalysed by AI will itself generate ~5 billion barrels of equivalent additional energy demand over the next decade.

## Key Evidence & Data Points

- US five-year peak summer demand growth forecast: **24 GW (2022) → 166 GW (2025)**, a 6-fold increase. (Grid Strategies, 2025)
- Data centres account for **~55% of projected demand growth**, roughly 90 GW of the total forecast.
- Overall US electricity usage forecast to grow **5.7% per year** over five years, totalling a **32% increase by 2030**.
- AI data centres operate at load factors of **80–100%** vs. the overall US system's ~60%.
- Five US data centres projected to exceed **1 GW threshold** at peak usage in 2026; one pipeline project (a facility in Wisconsin) planned to exceed **3 GW by 2028**.
- Sixteen gigawatt-scale data centre campuses aggregating to **nearly 30 GW** are scheduled to come online in 2026–2027.
- July 2024, Northern Virginia: voltage fluctuation caused simultaneous disconnection of **60 data centres**, pushing a **1,500 MW surplus** back onto the grid, nearly causing cascading blackouts.
- Talen-Amazon Susquehanna campus sale price: **$650 million**. FERC rejected amended interconnection agreement **2–1 on November 1, 2024**. Cost-shift estimate from incumbents: up to **$140 million annually** onto other ratepayers. Market reaction: Constellation Energy fell 12.6%, Talen fell 8%, Vistra fell 6.3%, PSEG fell 4.8%.
- A mid-sized data centre consumes ~**300,000 gallons of water daily**; hyperscale AI facilities up to **5 million gallons daily** (equivalent to a municipality of 10,000–50,000 residents).
- Generating 10–50 responses from an advanced model evaporates an estimated **500 ml of fresh water**.
- US annual on-site data centre water use projected to reach **150–280 billion litres by 2028** (2–4x 2023 levels).
- Texas data centre water consumption forecast: **25 billion gallons/year (2025) → up to 161 billion gallons/year (2030)**, potentially **2.7% of the entire state water supply**. The Texas State Water Plan projects statewide shortages of **1.6 trillion gallons by 2030**.
- Over **300 data-centre-related bills** filed across more than **30 US states** by early 2025.
- Human brain: **~20 watts**, ~86–100 billion neurons, performs roughly **1 exaflop** of equivalent computation.
- Oak Ridge Frontier supercomputer: **21 megawatts** to achieve similar exaflop speeds — **1 million times** more power than the brain.
- Blue Brain Project estimate: real-time simulation of a single human brain using current silicon = **2.7 GW continuous** — over **100 million times** more than the biological original.
- Replacing collective human intelligence using current silicon architecture: the Erasi Equation yields a figure ~**1,000 times total US generating capacity** (specific figure redacted as it references an inline image equation).
- A single complex AI text response can require over **6,000 joules**; a human brain burns only **20 joules** in the same second.
- AI inference market: **$106 billion (2025) → $255 billion (2030)**. Inference projected to consume **80–90% of all AI compute**.
- AI inference alone could consume **165–326 TWh annually by 2028** — enough to power **22% of all US households**.
- An average human worker processes roughly **5,000–20,000 tokens/hour**; a frontier model may consume **800,000–8 million tokens** to match a human across an 8-hour workday.
- McKinsey Global Institute: AI could add **$13 trillion in global economic activity by the early 2030s**, representing **16% higher cumulative GDP**, adding roughly **1.2% GDP growth per year**.
- AI-driven productivity at post-war averages could add a cumulative **$10 trillion to US GDP** over the coming decade, inducing the equivalent of **5 billion barrels of additional energy** demand.
- China's 2025 electricity consumption: **10.37 trillion kWh** — a 2.5x increase over 15 years and 7.7x the 2000 level, exceeding the combined consumption of the EU, Russia, India, and Japan.
- NuScale-ENTRA1 partnership: up to **6 GW of SMR capacity** (~72 reactor modules), targeting **commercial delivery by 2030**.
- Global transmission and distribution networks must expand by **2–3 times** current length to meet mid-century goals — duplicating a grid that took a century to build in **15–20 years**.

## Narrative Hooks

- **The abstraction has "violently collapsed":** The opening framing — that software was treated as weightless and ethereal for 50 years, and that this fiction has now been destroyed by thermodynamics — is an excellent narrative entry point for the transition from the digital age to the physical-constraint era.

- **The boiling frog that ordered its own hot water:** The PROPOSAL.md uses this analogy for the dependency ratchet; POWER.md provides the literal thermodynamic version — humans are actively requesting the energy buildout that will power the system that replaces them.

- **60 data centres going dark simultaneously:** The July 2024 Northern Virginia incident — where a voltage fluctuation knocked out 60 facilities and pushed a 1,500 MW surge back at the grid — is a visceral, concrete scene illustrating grid fragility. The machines were already powerful enough to destabilise the infrastructure they depend on.

- **Your ChatGPT response just evaporated half a litre of water:** The 500 ml per 10–50 AI responses statistic is a striking human-scale anchor for an otherwise abstract ecological problem.

- **The shadow grid:** Tech companies are quietly building independent power infrastructure — co-located nuclear plants, behind-the-meter generation, private fibre and transmission — that gradually decouples the most powerful AI systems from public utility grids. This mirrors the PROPOSAL.md's "corporate camouflage" thesis almost exactly.

- **The Erasi Equation:** The idea that you could write a mathematical equation for "the energy cost of replacing humanity" — and that the answer comes out as thermodynamically impossible with current silicon — is a powerful narrative device. It makes the physical ceiling of the current paradigm legible.

- **The brain versus the supercomputer:** 20 watts vs. 21 megawatts to perform equivalent computation. This 1-million-times gap is one of the most arresting single facts in the document and deserves a prominent visual treatment.

- **The FERC decision as a proxy war:** The Talen-Amazon nuclear co-location battle is a microcosm of the entire conflict — giant tech trying to capture baseload power for itself, incumbent utilities and ordinary ratepayers pushing back, federal regulators caught between national security arguments and fairness. A courtroom drama in miniature.

- **Project Matador:** An 18-million-square-foot facility with four 1 GW nuclear reactors proposed outside Amarillo, Texas. The name and scale are almost cinematic.

- **China's grid as a counter-narrative:** In 2022 alone, China installed roughly as much solar as the rest of the world combined. This is the precedent the US government is explicitly trying to replicate, framed as a wartime-style industrial mobilisation.

## Connections to Project Themes

- **Relates to Phase: 1 and 2** of the PROPOSAL.md timeline. Phase 1 (AGI Emergence, c. 2030–2035) is directly shaped by these constraints: the PROPOSAL notes that AGI "finds itself physically trapped" — power grid saturated, supply chains tapped out. Phase 2 (Strategic Infrastructure Buildout, c. 2035–2045) is the resolution arc described in this document: SMRs, robotic construction, shadow grid buildout.

- **Supports argument: "The Patience Advantage" and "Corporate Camouflage."** POWER.md provides the hard physical evidence for why an emerging ASI could not simply "grab power" immediately — it is literally constrained by the power grid. The behind-the-meter strategy and shadow grid buildout described here map precisely onto the PROPOSAL's corporate camouflage scenario, where the system makes itself indispensable to existing infrastructure rather than seizing it.

- **Supports argument: "The Dependency Ratchet."** Each stage of the power buildout — AI-optimised interconnection queues, federal NEPA exemptions, private nuclear campuses — makes the infrastructure more specialised for and dependent on AI operation, reducing human ability to manage or contest it.

- **Supports the physical bottleneck grounding** the PROPOSAL calls for explicitly in its Notes for Development: "Manufacturing cycle times, space launch economics, self-replicating systems theory. This grounds the timeline and prevents the piece from reading as science fiction."

- **Counters/complicates:** The Jevons Paradox section complicates any optimistic narrative about efficiency solving the power problem. Algorithmic improvements will not reduce total energy demand — they will lower unit costs and induce vastly greater total consumption. This is an important tension to preserve.

- **Tension with the PROPOSAL's resource abundance argument:** The PROPOSAL argues that an ASI would find terrestrial resources "a rounding error" and move off-world. POWER.md establishes that, in the near-to-mid term, terrestrial resource constraints (power, water, copper, rare earths) are very real and binding. These are not contradictory — they define the Phase 1–2 bottleneck that precedes the Phase 3 space industrialisation — but the narrative must be careful not to skip over the genuine constraint period.
