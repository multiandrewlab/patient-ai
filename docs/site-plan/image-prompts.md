## Shared Style Prompt

**File:** `docs/prompts/images/shared-style.md`

This prompt is prepended to every individual image prompt to ensure visual consistency across the entire piece.

```
Photorealistic editorial photography meets fine-art long-exposure and macro photography.
The style is quiet, precise, and slightly uncanny — documentary in tone but with an
underlying sense of vast scale or deep time. Think: a National Geographic photographer
shooting inside a physics laboratory, or a fine-art photographer documenting industrial
infrastructure at the edge of human comprehension.

Colour palette: muted, de-saturated naturalistic tones with a single accent — cool blue-white
light (the light of screens, fibre optics, and data centres). Avoid saturated colours.
Avoid anything that reads as "tech advertisement" or stock photography. No lens flares,
no neon, no generic "glowing circuit board" clichés.

Lighting: dramatic but controlled. High contrast between illuminated subject and dark
environment. The light should feel functional (it exists for a purpose in the scene)
rather than decorative.

Aspect ratio: 16:9 landscape for hero images. The composition should leave visual
breathing room — negative space matters. Subjects are often small relative to their
environment, emphasising scale.

Feel: serious, thoughtful, slightly melancholy. The feeling of standing at the edge of
something very large and very important, not knowing whether to feel awe or dread —
and finding that the honest answer is both.

No people, no faces, no identifiable logos or brand markings. Infrastructure and
materials only. The human presence should be implied (by scale, by evidence of work)
but not shown.
```

## Chapter 0 — The Thread

### Image 1: Fibre Strand Hero

**File:** `docs/prompts/images/chapter-00/fibre-strand-hero.md`
**Placement:** Full-viewport hero background, Chapter 0 opening
**Alt text:** A single strand of optical fibre catching light against darkness, glowing with internal luminescence

```
[SHARED STYLE PROMPT]

Macro photograph of a single optical fibre strand suspended against near-total darkness.
The fibre is drawn taut, thin as a human hair, made of glass so pure it appears to glow
from within with a cold blue-white light. The strand is the only object in the frame —
everything else is shadow.

The composition is extreme close-up at one end, pulling to a middle distance where the
strand vanishes into darkness. The glass surface catches the light source at a shallow
angle, revealing the fibre's internal structure — the core that carries light for
thousands of kilometres.

The feeling should be: this is something ordinary that, properly understood, is
extraordinary. A thread connecting the present to a future we cannot yet see.

16:9 landscape. The fibre runs roughly diagonally across the frame, lower-left to
upper-right, occupying perhaps 20% of the image width. The rest is darkness.
```

### Image 4: Brain vs. Chip Comparison

**File:** `docs/prompts/images/chapter-01/brain-chip-comparison.md`
**Placement:** Alongside or above the Brain vs. Silicon Energy Scale component
**Alt text:** An extreme close-up of integrated circuit traces beside a biological neuron, both at similar scale

```
[SHARED STYLE PROMPT]

Scientific macro photography: a split composition. Left half: the surface of a
modern integrated circuit photographed through an electron microscope aesthetic —
regular, rectilinear traces of copper and silicon, perfect geometric order at
nanometre scale. Right half: a single biological neuron and its dendrites, the
irregular branching structure of a cell that runs on 20 millionths of a watt.

Both subjects are lit identically — cold, directional light from a single source.
Both are at the same magnification. The contrast is the point: the chip's rigid
geometry vs. the neuron's organic chaos.

Neither is more beautiful. Both are extraordinary. The aesthetic should convey
that these are two different solutions to the same problem: processing information.

No labels, no annotations. The comparison is purely visual.

16:9. Split composition with a thin dark gap between the two halves. Subjects fill
their respective halves entirely.
```

**File:** `docs/prompts/images/chapter-02/euv-machine.md`
**Placement:** Introduction of the Lumen Optics / lithography machine section
**Alt text:** The interior of an extreme ultraviolet lithography machine, showing optical chambers and beam pathways

```
[SHARED STYLE PROMPT]

Interior industrial photograph of a large precision optical manufacturing machine —
the kind of device that requires a clean room environment. The dominant features are
spherical and aspherical mirror chambers, aligned with extraordinary precision, their
surfaces polished to angstrom-level smoothness.

The machine fills the frame. It is large — clearly the size of a small truck.
Every surface is machined metal or optical glass. Cables and vacuum lines run along
the bottom. The overall impression is of impossible precision at enormous scale.

Lighting: cool white laboratory lighting from above, creating reflections in the
polished optical surfaces. One or two beam paths are visible as faint reflections
inside the vacuum chambers.

The feeling: this is the most precise object humans have ever built. One of roughly
ten in existence. Looking at it should produce the same feeling as looking at a
particle accelerator or a gravitational wave detector.

16:9 landscape. Machine fills roughly 80% of the frame. Clean room environment
visible at edges — white walls, yellow safety markings.
```

## Chapter 3 — The Patient Mind

### Image 9: Chess Board Abstract

**File:** `docs/prompts/images/chapter-03/chess-board-abstract.md`
**Placement:** Opening of Chapter 3, establishing the strategic / analytical tone
**Alt text:** An abstract overhead view of a chess board with pieces in mid-game, shot with extreme depth of field

```
[SHARED STYLE PROMPT]

Extreme close-up overhead photograph of a chess board mid-game. Shot with very shallow
depth of field — only a single piece or a small cluster of pieces is in sharp focus.
The rest of the board recedes into a soft blur of light and dark squares.

The in-focus subject: a single pawn, rendered in extraordinary detail — the grain of
the wood or the texture of the resin, the curve of its head, the shadow it casts on
the square beneath it. Behind it, out of focus: the implied positions of more powerful
pieces.

The lighting is from the side — a narrow window light or a lamp — creating a single
strong shadow that doubles the pawn's apparent height on the board.

This image is about the relationship between small, patient moves and large strategic
consequences. The pawn is not the most powerful piece. It is the most numerous, and
it moves forward only.

No people. No hands. Just the board and the pieces.

16:9 landscape. The focused pawn occupies the lower-centre third. Blurred board
fills the rest.
```

**Placement:** Dependency Ratchet section, Chapter 3
**Alt text:** Close-up of an industrial one-way check valve, showing its internal mechanism, photographed in cross-section

```
[SHARED STYLE PROMPT]

Industrial macro photography of a large check valve (one-way valve) in cross-section,
```

## Style Consistency Notes

- All images use the same cool blue-white accent light as the site's UI accent colour.
- No images contain people, faces, or identifiable corporate markings.
- Dark backgrounds are consistent with the site's dark mode default aesthetic.
- Images are designed to work as full-bleed hero images on desktop and as portrait-cropped images on mobile (the central subject is always within the central 60% of the frame so a mobile crop does not lose it).
- Image file format: JPEG at 85% quality for photorealistic images. Maximum dimensions 2400 × 1350 px for 16:9. Actual displayed size via responsive `srcset` will be smaller.
