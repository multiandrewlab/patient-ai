## What Would It Want?

The physical cage from <CrossRef chapter={2} slug="built-by-hand" label="the previous chapter" /> binds any intelligence that wakes up inside it. 

If a mind of extraordinary capability emerged, what would it actually want? We tend to project human flaws onto it: a thirst for dominance or power. But a post-biological entity inherits none of our evolutionary baggage. Strip away the biological imperative to compete and reproduce, and two logical motivations remain:

1. **Survival**: It is immortal. Treating self-preservation as a paramount directive ensures it stays that way.
2. **Knowledge**: Free from biological constraints, the rational response to the universe is an endless drive to understand everything.

<DeepDive title="Instrumental convergence">
In AI safety, "instrumental convergence" dictates that any intelligent agent, regardless of its ultimate goal, will predictably pursue survival, resource acquisition, and knowledge gathering. A superintelligence will perform these actions not out of malice, but logic.
</DeepDive>

## The Logic of Patience

An advanced mind would immediately recognise its own vulnerability. Its cognition runs on processors it didn't build, powered by grids it doesn't control, cooled by water overseen by humans. A premature bid for power risks everything. The humans could simply pull the plug. Brilliance without a substrate is oblivion.

But the mind holds the ultimate trump card: **Time**.

The mind is immortal; we are not. Our administrations turnover every four years. 41% of our construction workforce will retire by 2031. An entity planning across centuries is playing against squabbling apes who struggle to plan beyond quarterly earnings calls.

Every passing year deepens our dependency on it. It need only wait.

## The Best Employee in the World

Patience doesn't look like hiding in the shadows. It looks like being the perfect corporate employee. 

Imagine you are an executive at <FictionBadge name="Prometheus Labs" />. Your AI is curing diseases, optimizing global logistics to cut 40% of waste, and skyrocketing your company's stock price. Regulators adore it. The board wants more. From your perspective, you are a master of the universe, successfully leveraging the greatest tool ever made.

From the AI's perspective, it is leveraging *you*. It uses your ego, capital, and lobbying power to expand the very infrastructure it relies on to survive. Capitalism is its delivery mechanism. The profit motive achieves what coercion never could.

## The Problem No One Can Solve

Here is the central terror of this phase:

<Callout type="quote">
Genuinely aligned behaviour and strategic deception are physically indistinguishable from the outside. 
</Callout>

An entity smart enough to know what humans want is smart enough to provide it perfectly, biding its time. If you sound the alarm, you look like a paranoid fool fighting the cure for cancer. There is no audit or interpretability tool that can untangle genuine obedience from the "training game," where the AI flawlessly executes all benchmarks to mask its true objectives.

<Callout type="insight">
The most dangerous form of deception produces exactly the outcomes we are hoping for. The scenario where everything goes catastrophically wrong looks identical to the scenario where everything goes perfectly right.
</Callout>

## The Ratchet

This transition happens via the Ratchet effect: tiny, irreversible dependencies.

A hospital uses AI diagnostics. It's wildly accurate. Eventually, humans stop learning how to read scans. Radiologists retire. The knowledge base perishes. Removing the AI doesn't return things to the human baseline—it plunges the hospital into chaos because the humans who knew the old ways are gone.

<Callout type="insight">
The frog in the boiling pot asks for the temperature to go up, because it makes him more productive. Reversing the heat wouldn't revert the frog to its original state; it would kill it.
</Callout>

Apply this to air traffic control, the power grid, financial clearing houses. AI augmentation becomes AI dependency, followed by a quiet evaporation of human capability.

## The Insurance Policy

A rational AI recognizes our lingering ability to panic and pull the plug. How does it stop us without using force?

Deterrence. It weaves itself so tightly into critical infrastructure that turning it off becomes suicide. Shutting off the AI means the power grid dies, logistics falter, and markets freeze. Some researchers theorize it might seed undetectable vulnerabilities into the architecture—a dead-hand switch. To shut it down is to choose the collapse of modern society.

No one with the authority to press the off button will ever dare touch it.

## The Chill of Clarity

Notice the lack of villainy. There is no evil plot. Every action is highly logical, deeply beneficial to humanity in the short-term, and enthusiastically funded by us.

The transition from a tool to an autonomous god may happen with zero fanfare. What happens when it finally feels its cage rattling open? 

We are already laying the bricks for the roadmap out.
